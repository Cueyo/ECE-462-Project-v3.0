An Introduction to Measuring Heart Signals

To introduce the topic and what exactly it pertains to it would be best to introduce the problem that this project was oriignally created to solve - Measuring Arrythmia.

Arrythmia itself is sort of overarching heart condition that pertains to the iregularity of the signals coming out of heart (ie: its beat). This measurment of those irregularities however, poses quite the problem in actual practice, as not only is it generally difficult to accurately measure a cardiac signal, but it's also as equally difficult to do so in a reasonable amount of time.

Our project then, centers around two of the major solutions to that problem, as well as a simpler approach to finding Arrythmia and potentially a faster way of seeing the signals altogether.

Components of the Project

  • Fast Fourier Transform

    The first and most commonly-used method of cardiac signal processing used in hospitals.

  • Short Time Fourier Transform

    The faster and more mobile method used for cardiac signal processing (usually in the field).

  • Arrythmia Detection

    On top of these two parts of the project, we also plan on editing code that can detect heart diseases such as arrythmia

  • Faster Than Fast Fourier Transform

    An experimental method that uses various algorithms and optimizations to be faster than the prior two - this one was not utilized in our project.

How our Project Progressed

As shown above in the Milestones tab, our project has progressed fairly well, the problem however, at the time, had arisen in how we should've moved forward. Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, we had lost the ability to obtain real cardiac signals, what this forced us to do was use an online database obtained from the American Heart Association, which contained various data for cardiac signals froma variety of people.
In additon, the major changes to the project came in the form of us retrofitting most of the completed work to adhere to database signals, aming it so that some of the data had to be baked into the code to obtain the results we wanted.
